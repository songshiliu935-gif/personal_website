01 / About

Econometrician, Data Scientist
& Mathematician

I am an Applied Econometrician, Data Scientist, and Mathematician based at the University of Melbourne. My academic journey is driven by a profound interest in Time Series Analysis, Causal Inferences, Macroeconomics, and Advanced Machine Learning. By integrating modern ML tools with traditional economic frameworks, I aim to uncover the causal mechanisms that drive markets, policy, and human behaviour.

I believe that great econometrics is as much an art as a science: choosing the right identification strategy, stress-testing assumptions, and communicating uncertainty honestly are skills that take a lifetime to refine.

02 / Expertise

Core Areas of Expertise

☷

Causal Inference

Mastery of identification strategies including Instrumental Variables, Difference-in-Differences, Regression Discontinuity, and Synthetic Control methods.

▽

Time-Series Analysis

Extensive work with VAR, VECM, ARCH/GARCH, and state-space models for macroeconomic forecasting and financial econometrics.

△

Microeconometrics

Panel data methods, discrete choice models, sample selection corrections, and treatment effect heterogeneity in applied micro contexts.

◆

Bayesian Methods

Bayesian VAR, hierarchical models, and MCMC simulation — bringing probabilistic thinking to structural economic questions.

■

Policy Evaluation

Designing and executing quasi-experimental evaluations of labour market, health, and education policies for government clients.

◯

Machine Learning × Econometrics

Integrating modern ML tools (LASSO, Random Forests, Double ML) with structural economic frameworks for high-dimensional settings.
